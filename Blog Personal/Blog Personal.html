Mount Fuji, The Sleeping Vulcano

Mount Fuji is the highest mountain in Japan (3776 meters), located about 100 kilometers away from Tokyo. Mount Fuji has the shape of a perfectly symmetrical cone and is a famous symbol of Japan, with frequent appearances in photos and tourism advertisements. In Japanese culture, Mount Fiji is considered a holy place, an element of nature that Japan has been blessed with by divinity.

Sanja Matsuri Festival Tokyo

Sanja Festivalor Asakusa Sanja Festival is other of the three great Shinto festivals in Tokyo held annually for 3 days on the third weekend of May in Asakusa Shrine. Is considered for some people as one of the biggest and wildest festival in Tokyo, with over 2 million visitors in 3 days.The festival is held in honor of Hinokuma Hamanari, Hinokuma Takenari and Hajino Nakatomo, the three men who founded Senso-ji temple. Senso-ji temple (浅草寺) is a Buddhist temple located in Asakusa and it’s the oldest temple in Tokyo, and dates back to 645, the date on which was completed.
